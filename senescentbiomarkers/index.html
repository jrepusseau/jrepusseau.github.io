Senescence cell biomarkers

Cellular senescence is linked to a number of predictable phenotypic traits detectable by specific biomarkers. However, due to the heterogeneity of senescence cells and senescence inducing, a combination of multiple techniques are needed to spot senescent cells.


Senescence-Associated Secretory phenotype (SASP)

Study on SASP is one of the most promising factor for senescent cell detection and characterization.Firstly characterized using biased antibodies array, new unbiased way such as SOMAscan have shown the complexity and heterogeneity of SASP composition. By the development of a specific of a proteomic database studies in both IR, RAS, ATV induced senescent in fibroblast cell. SOMAscan highlight that few soluble SASP biomarkers, plasminogen activator inhibitor-1 (SERPINE1), C-X-C motif ligand 1 (CXCL1) ,matrix metallic-proteinases (MMP1), stamiocalcin 1 (STC1) and growth factors 15 (GDF15) were represented in both IR, RAS, ATV induced senescent cell. Notably, quantification of GDF15 by western blotting allows to create a proteomic signature of age more precise than the ‘epigenetic clock’. SASP also contains extracellular vesicles EV that can be divided in exosomes (endosomal membrane) and micro vesicles (plasma membrane). Observed both in IR, RAS and ATV senescence induced fibroblast cells. Plasma membrane components have been intensively evaluated as efficient senescent biomarkers (concanavalin A, fucose, glucosamine). Among them (ICAM-1, NOTCH3, DCR2) have been discovered then studies find DEP1, B2MG and DPP4 have reliable PM biomarkers. Another study showed that expression of vimentin, which is highly expressed in senescent cells, could be efficiently targeted by using natural antibodies such as 9H04. Few differences have been made observing senescence inducer and SASP composition. Fibroblast RS, RAS, X-radiation induced senescence remain unchanged most of SASP interleukin then express close SASP. Fibroblast endothelial induced by oncogene RAS or dysfunctional p53 secrete more inflammatory factors and growth factors than other senescence pathways. Over-expression of p16 or p21 in embryonic senescence cells does not express SASP.


SASP studying also shows differences between cells type. Comparing two IR-induced senescent cells SASP proteins, renal epithelial were enriched in pathways on cell structure, mobility and depleted in RNA metabolism compared to fibroblast cell. Renal epithelial cell also observe a decrease in DAMP such as a previously studied HMGB-1 (High Mobility Group Box 1)


Following DNA damage, DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) will recruit ATM kinase to damaged sites, which drives phosphorylation of H2AX. Persistent DNA damage markers (yH2AX, 53BPI, Rad17, ATR, ATM, Mre11,MDC1,CHK2) are also senescent biomarkers part of the SASP.

SIR

Senescent inflammatory responses (SIR) also characterized senescent cells by a unique phenotype. SIR is partly related to SASP and both share common compounds (CD9, CXCL1, CXCL5, ICAM-1, 16F2BPI, itga2, PTGES). However, SIR have no paracrine role and only to report senescent cell damages controlled by different transcription regulators.

TASP

Studying telomere-associated secretory phenotype (TASP) secreted from telomeres dysfonctionnement can also bring additional information than SASP. Among them Cathelicidin-related antimicrobial protein (CRAMP), Chitinase 3 like protein 3 (Chi3L3), Elongation factor 1a (EF1a) and stathmin have shown to give complete diagnosis on senescence cells. EF-1a and stathmin secretion are specific regarding organs cells where CRAMP and chitinase enzyme have shown better correlation with human aging than IL-6 study.

Change in mitochondrial functions

Chromatin remodeling may be at the origin of irreversible senescent state in cells. In cell, ATP-dependent chromatin assembly factors (ACP) dysfunctionality, will lead to chromatin remodeling and the inducing of senescent state. Low level of BAZ1A, part of ACP, has been observed in senescent cells where cancerous cells have shown excessive levels of BAZ1A. Accumulation of mitochondria is detected mitotrackers to measure the membranes potential of mitochondria and electronic microscopy to evaluate their cell shape (fusion/fission) have been used in gene studies


Increased of lysosomal content in senescent cell have increased the Senescence-associated beta-galactosidase (SA-β-gal or SABG) which can be asset using fluorescence based staining or quantitative test at pH 6.0. However SA-β-gal also increases in quiescence cell and in and response to several stress then not specific to senescent cells. Sudan Black B (SBB), biotin analog (GL13) or orange acridine can be used to detect lysosomal mass from old lysosomes and lysotracker of to reveal high lysosomal content.


In senescent High ROS production using the oxidation of 2’-7’ dichlorofluorescin (H2DCF) to 2’-7’ dichlorofluorescein (DCF) a fluorescent dying agent. H2O2, one of biggest components of reactive oxygen species reacts with the H2DCF ferrous ion to get reduced in H2O following Fenton reaction. Enhanced by the production of DCF, fluorescence can then measured and directly correlated with ROS production. As well as SA-β-gal, high ROS production isnt specific to senescence and affected in cancerous cell, differentiation.

DNA Synthesis

Using H-thymidine, 5-bromo-2-deoxyuridine (BrdU) or 5-ethynyl-2-deoxyuridine (EdU) have shown to induce senescence in cells. Thus, by measuring the incorporation rate of BrdU or EdU nucleotides assays we can confirm DNA replication is near absent, then that cell observed are actually senescent.

epigenetic clock

Cell cycle arrest with the expression of genes pRb, p16, p53 are relatively robust markers of senescence as their continuous expression is required to induce cell cycle arrest. p21 constant expression have been shown to be more specifically linked to embryonic senescence. The ‘Epigenetic clock’ a biomarker index that combines weighted information of a subset of DNA methylation sites then is an indirect senescent cell biomarkers.

Senescence-cell anti-apoptotic pathways SCAP

BCL-2 protein family BCL-2, BCL-W, BCL- XL, MCL-1 and A1 play a central role as anti-apoptotic factors. Quantitative real time analysis revealed that BCL-W variant 2 detected in all senescent. BCL-W variant 1 mRNA level elevated in RS. BCL-XL was only elevated in OIS.

Morphology

Senescent cells display a large and flat morphology with a nuclear enlargement that can be quantitatively assessed using imaging cytometry, such as provided by laser scanning cytometer (LSC). LSC is the microscope-based quantitative-image analyzer that offers the combined advantages of flow cytometry and image analysis. Then measurement of nuclear size (area) over the intensity of maximal pixel of DNA-associated fluorescence per nucleus, after DNA staining with fluorescent dyes such as 4,6-diamidino-2-phenylindole (DAPI), that provides a convenient marker of the degree (‘depth’) of senescent cells. Plasma membrane mechanics change regarding solidity or cell size can be evaluated regarding electrophysiology techniques, biophysical techniques (tangential flow filtration).
