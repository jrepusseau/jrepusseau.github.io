Senescence properties

Senescence is a dynamic multistep process that conducts cells to cycle arrest. The senescent cells undergo change in viability, large size/ irregular shape, growth arrest, loss of lamin B1, resistance to apoptosis and also at the subcellular level regarding p16, p21 gene activation, SA Bgal activity , lipofuscin accumulation, CCL autophagic signal modulation and secrete senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP) .

Senescence can occur through intrinsic and extrinsic conditions. Hayflick and Moorhead first described replicative senescent (RS). Through multiple division (40-60) telomere erosion leads to RS, which is the most observed source of senescence. Senescence can also occur from cellular stress inducing DNA damage response (DDR). All cells experiment in vivo DDR and respond to a transient repairable damage of p53. Persistent DDR from intrinsic (oxidative damage, telome attrition, hyperproliferation), external insults (UV, y-irradiation, ROS contributors) or nutrition depletion cause stress induced premature senescence (SIPS). Activation of a sarcoma viral oncogene homolog (RAS) increases proliferation rate direct causes of DNA damage where activation of v-raf murine sarcoma viral oncogene homolog B (BRAF) involves p16 induction, up regulation of the inflammatory interleukins IL-6, IL-8 and activation of pyruvate dehydrogenase (PDH). PDH activation then leads to increased mitochondrial metabolism, which in turn increases ROS production then DDR. RAS and BRAF are part of the oncogene induced senescence (OIS). The DDR associated with RS depends on the telomere length, where the DDR associated with OIS,SIPS is related to telomere dysfunction.

DDR stimuli will activate the p53/p21 axis inducing senescence or apoptosis in order to prevent cancer cell formation. In the nucleus, p53 induces the transcription of the cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitor p21, which in turn blocks CDK2 activity , resulting in hypophosphorylated retinoblastoma proteins (Rb). p53/p21 signalling will also increase ROS production inducing DNA damages and causing the cell cycle arrest. p21 constant signalisation has been found in embryonic senescence in an independent way from DDR. The INK4/ARF locus triggers cell cycle arrest/senescence and apoptosis by encoding three tumor suppressors, ARF and p16, encoded by CDKN2A, and p15 , encoded by CDKN2B. DDR activates serine/threonine kinase ATM which are proteins responsible to check DNA damage, leading to cell cycle arrest, DNA repair or apoptosis. In case ATM checkpoints are disabled and the oncogenic stimuli increase ARF is activated to preserve p53 degradation from DDR by either inducing senescence or apoptotic. p15 and p16 selectively inhibit CDK4 and CDK6 that keeps the Rb hypophosphorylated and enforcing the G1 arrest. While p53/p21 induced the senescent response, p16 maintains the senescence process in the cell. By p38 mediation, p16 locks the cell in senescence via a constant oxidative stress and generation of DDR. p16 mutations leading to senescence evasion may be the most frequent cause of malignant cell development. DDR triggers the biosynthesis of SASP regulated by mammalian targets of rapamycin (mTOR). The first complex of mTOR (mTORC1) interplay with endoplasmic reticulum (ER) to secure protein production and maintain senescent cell anti-apoptotic pathways (SCAP). mTORC1 increases the biosynthesis and leads to accumulation of misfolded/unfolded proteins and so the demand for ER mitochondries. ER stress will directly inhibit AKT, a survival kinase responsible for autophagy signaling. Autophagy increases the dysfunctional mitochondria leading to oxidative phosphorylation and endogenous ROS formation. Conversely, prolonged ER stress induces the activation of the P13K pathway leading to an activation AKT, inhibition in autophagy and mTORC1 activity.

DDR signaling activate several agent responsible for SASP production such as nuclear factor kB (NF-kβ), mitochondrial dysfunction-associated senescence (MiDAS), GATA4, and CCAAT/enhancer-binding protein-β (C/EBPβ). NF-kβ and C/EBPβ mainly regulate SASP by controlling inflammatory interleukins (IL-4) and (IL-8). SASP factors can be globally divided into soluble factors (interleukins, chemokines, growth factors and secreted proteases) and secreted insoluble proteins / ECM components. Pro inflammatory and immunomodulatory cytokines and chemokines include interleukins (IL-6, IL-7, IL-8), monocyte chemoattractant protein (MCP-2) and macrophage inflammatory protein (MIP)-3a. Secreted growth factors include growth Regulated Oncogene alpha (GROa), hepatocyte growth factor (HGF), insulin-like growth factor binding protein (IGFBP) and . Among secreted proteases, matrix metalloproteinases (MMPs) family and serine proteases (ICAMs, uPAR, TNF receptors). ECM components include collagens and miminin.

Senescence mechanism limits the replication of old and damaged cells in order to prevent malignancy by blocking replication. Senescent cells also contribute to wound healing and resolution. The SASP plays a major role in cancer proliferation. Growth factors notably increase tumor growth and vascularization where factors such as MMP-2 can also lead to inhibition of anti-tumour T-cell responses thus premalignant and malignant cells immune evasion. Also SASP induces senescence to neighbours cells in an autocrine and paracrine manner. Accumulation of senescent cell drive aging and age related disease backed from the systematic observation of SC in age related disease and the selective killing of SC (senotherapy) decrease aging and age-related disease.
