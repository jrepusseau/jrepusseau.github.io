Bring my


father home

On December 11th 1969, Mr. Hwang’s father, Hwang Won, boarded the Korean Air YS-11 flight to go on a business trip. Within 10 minutes, his plane was hijacked by a North Korean agent. While 39 passengers were returned, 11 abductees, including Mr. Hwang’s father, were not. \n \n Team Hwang is a group of volunteers who have come together to join Mr Hwang in raising international awareness of this case and help bring his father back home to South Korea. \n \n Crimes of forced abduction committed by North Korea are vast in number and ongoing. By raising our voices, we will help pave the way for resolving hundreds of other abduction cases committed by North Korea.

Latest news

  • 2019/02/18 Amnesty International has joined us in our fight to bring Hwang Won home and released a petition addressed to President Moon Jae-in, demanding that he include the issue of forced abductions in the next inter-Korean summit
  • 2019/03 Hwang In-cheol and project manager, Kwon Youngmin, travelled to Geneva for the UN Human Rights Council’s 40th session to attend meetings and advocate with UN member states. Prior to their trip to Geneva, they went to Brussels to deliver a speech at the European Parliament.
  • 2019/03 The EU and UN trip was a great success. On May 9th, during North Korea's 3rd cycle UPR (Universal Periodic Review), the UN Human Rights Council was made fully aware of the KAL abduction victims. Uruguay and Iceland both highlighted the KAL hijacking incident in their human rights recommendations to North Korea. Iceland even explicitly mentioned Hwang Won's name, demanding that North Korea immediately return him to South Korea.

Where and what can I find?


Datasets: to follow the evolution of ice caps of from the two poles: Arctic and Antarctic.

NASA ArcticDEM Project: ArcticDEM is a collaborative project focusing on producing high quality digital model of Arctic with the help of stereoscopic imagery tool. In other words, you can use this project to search for images of the South Pole ice levels and distribution. You can find more details here: ArcticDEM Project.

.
NASA IceBridge DEM: Ongoing mission focusing on monitor the changes of polar ice. Nasa uses a plane instead of satellites to map and store data in a specific zone. With the help of sensors, it measures the elevation of the ice surface, earth presence, vegetaion and ice. Below, for example you can see the trajectory of IceBridge planes.
LVIS (Land, Vegetation and Ice Sensor) : It's the sensor for the project IceBridge and it allows to measure the height of vegetation in the polar regions with the help of water. Moreover, it gives all the data related to the vegetation in less accessible parts for humans. You can find more details here: LVIS

.
AIRGrav: Developed by Sander Geophysics, the only gravimeter transportable by air. It was designed at the base for oil exploration, where it represents a cheaper alternative to land and maritim surveys. AIRGrav gravimeter uses several sensitive gyroscopes to keep the instrument orientation stable. You can find more information here: AIRGrav
