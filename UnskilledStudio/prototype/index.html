Arctic Antarctica Issue

   Universally, Arctic is represented by his winter coat at the North Pole. It includes 6 countries: Canada, USA (Alaska), Danemark (Groenland), Russia, Norway and Iceland. All 6 with Sweden and Finland represent the Arctic Consulate.

   Research on that continent is vital for humanity, mostly because of its ecologic impact. More people are concerned and understand about, more powerful will be the action to save this part of the planet.


Arctic represents:
  • In average between 1979-2010 : 6,6 millions km²
  • In minimal low extent in september 2012 : 3,55 millions km²
  • Every year since 1966 we've been loosing in average 290 gigatons of ice from the ice cap of Greenland
    Even though most part of the ice transfers to Antarctic, there is significant percentage that is lost through the way. Moreover, last years temperatures surpassing the usual values from 20 to 30 degrees Celsius has been seen several times in Arctic. End of December 2015, North pole has seen positive temperatures, while the normal temperature for this period in 1958-2002 was -28 degrees Celsius.
   Antarctica is Earth's southernmost continent. It contains the geographic South Pole and is situated in the Antarctic region of the Southern Hemisphere, almost entirely south of the Antarctic Circle, and is surrounded by the Southern Ocean. Alternatively, it may be considered to be surrounded by the southern Pacific, Atlantic, and Indian Oceans, or by the southern waters of the World Ocean.

   Antarctica, the fifth-largest continent:
  • It is 14,000,000 square kilometres (5,400,000 square miles). For comparison, Antarctica is nearly twice the size of Australia.
  • Antarctica is smaller than Asia, Africa or America but taller than Europe and Oceania.


   A continent covered by ice:
  • About 98% of Antarctica is covered by ice that averages 1.9 km (1.2 mi; 6,200 ft)in thickness, which extends to all but the northernmost reaches of the Antarctic Peninsula.
  • The coastline measures 17,968 km (11,165 mi) and is mostly characterised by ice formations.
  • Antarctica, on average, is the coldest, driest, and windiest continent, and has the highest average elevation of all the continents.
  • Antarctica is a desert, with annual precipitation of only 200 mm (8 in) along the coast and far less inland.

   A frozen continent:
  • Average temperature for the third quarter of the year: (the coldest part of the year)is −63 °C (−81 °F).
  • Temperature record : −89.2 °C (−128.6 °F) (or even −94.7 °C (−135.8 °F) as measured from space).

   Many scientists are in Antarctica:
  • Anywhere from 1,000 to 5,000 people reside throughout the year at research stations scattered across the continent.
  • Each year, scientists from 28 different nations conduct experiments not reproducible in any other place in the world. In the summer more than 4,000 scientists operate research stations; this number decreases to just over 1,000 in the winter.
  • Researchers include biologists, geologists, oceanographers, physicists, astronomers, glaciologists, and meteorologists.
  • Glaciologists in Antarctica are concerned with the study of the history and dynamics of floating ice, seasonal snow, glaciers, and ice sheets.
   Officially, Antarctica can't be part of any state, even if some of them may claim otherwise.
   

Lack of knowledge related to the poles


   To accentuate the lack of this knowledge, we can just ask where do the bears live: Arctic or Antarctic? Arctic comes from old greek word "Arktos", which translates as "bear". Consequently, Antarctic would suggest that it's the place without bears. Even more, do you know which one is in the South and which one is in the North?

   Therefore, we think that people should be able to learn and understand more about the poles, especially with their great impact on our future. For instance, 3/4 of the fresh water is held in the form of ice about 90% of which is in the Antarctic.

   As NASA is studying this field intensively by using various tools, like satellites and on place researchers, it provides tremendous amount of data. Therefore our main goal is to make this information accessible everywhere to everyone.

Where and what can I find?


Datasets: to follow the evolution of ice caps of from the two poles: Arctic and Antarctic.

NASA ArcticDEM Project: ArcticDEM is a collaborative project focusing on producing high quality digital model of Arctic with the help of stereoscopic imagery tool. In other words, you can use this project to search for images of the South Pole ice levels and distribution. You can find more details here: ArcticDEM Project.

.
NASA IceBridge DEM: Ongoing mission focusing on monitor the changes of polar ice. Nasa uses a plane instead of satellites to map and store data in a specific zone. With the help of sensors, it measures the elevation of the ice surface, earth presence, vegetaion and ice. Below, for example you can see the trajectory of IceBridge planes.
LVIS (Land, Vegetation and Ice Sensor) : It's the sensor for the project IceBridge and it allows to measure the height of vegetation in the polar regions with the help of water. Moreover, it gives all the data related to the vegetation in less accessible parts for humans. You can find more details here: LVIS

.
AIRGrav: Developed by Sander Geophysics, the only gravimeter transportable by air. It was designed at the base for oil exploration, where it represents a cheaper alternative to land and maritim surveys. AIRGrav gravimeter uses several sensitive gyroscopes to keep the instrument orientation stable. You can find more information here: AIRGrav
