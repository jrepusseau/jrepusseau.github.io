Bring my


father home


BBC link

On December 11th 1969, Mr. Hwang’s father, Hwang Won, boarded the Korean Air YS-11 flight to go on a business trip. Within 10 minutes, his plane was hijacked by a North Korean agent. While 39 passengers were returned, 11 abductees, including Mr. Hwang’s father, were not. \n \n Team Hwang is a group of volunteers who have come together to join Mr Hwang in raising international awareness of this case and help bring his father back home to South Korea. \n \n Crimes of forced abduction committed by North Korea are vast in number and ongoing. By raising our voices, we will help pave the way for resolving hundreds of other abduction cases committed by North Korea.

Latest news

  • 2019/02/18 Amnesty International has joined us in our fight to bring Hwang Won home and released a petition addressed to President Moon Jae-in, demanding that he include the issue of forced abductions in the next inter-Korean summit
  • 2019/03 Hwang In-cheol and project manager, Kwon Youngmin, travelled to Geneva for the UN Human Rights Council’s 40th session to attend meetings and advocate with UN member states. Prior to their trip to Geneva, they went to Brussels to deliver a speech at the European Parliament.
  • 2019/04 The EU and UN trip was a great success. On May 9th, during North Korea's 3rd cycle UPR (Universal Periodic Review), the UN Human Rights Council was made fully aware of the KAL abduction victims. Uruguay and Iceland both highlighted the KAL hijacking incident in their human rights recommendations to North Korea. Iceland even explicitly mentioned Hwang Won's name, demanding that North Korea immediately return him to South Korea.

Goals


By joining Hwang In-cheol in his efforts to bring his father home, we are standing up against impunity and North Korea’s crimes against humanity.



Find out about the fate and whereabouts of Hwang Won


North Korea continues to deny Hwang In-cheol access to any information on his father’s current whereabouts. Despite having made numerous requests (e.g., inter-Korean family reunions, letters to ICRC and UN) for confirmation of his father’s vital status, Pyongyang continually refuses to verify whether he is alive or not.


Team Hwang aims to mobilize grassroots movement in support of Hwang In-cheol so that North Korea has no choice but to disclose correct information about his father’s situation.



Secure Hwang Won’s right to return to South Korea


Everyone has the right to freely stay or leave his or her country, and to be with one’s family. That right has been blatantly violated for Hwang Won and his family for the last 50 years.


Team Hwang aims to uphold these basic rights and reunite a father with his family.



Raise awareness, both domestically and internationally, of the 1969 hijacking of Korean Air YS-11 flight


This is the only plane hijacking case in history that remains unresolved, which is largely due to lack of awareness and interest. Team Hwang recalls similar cases of North Korean abductions that were successfully resolved as a result of widespread awareness and a willingness to take action.

(See ‘Other abductions’ page)

Team Hwang aims to accumulate worldwide pressure on North Korea to investigate this crime and provide relief to Hwang Won and the other hijacking victims.



Raise awareness, both domestically and internationally, of the forced abductions committed by North Korea


North Korea’s abductions remain an ongoing issue that targets many countries, including in Asia, the Middle East, North America and Europe.


Team Hwang hopes that by raising awareness of Hwang Won’s case, we will pave the way to solving hundreds of other abduction cases.



Ensure accountability


Since 1983, North Korea has been a state party to the Convention for the Suppression of Unlawful Seizure of Aircraft, under which anyone who hijacks a civilian aircraft should be extradited or prosecuted “without exception whatsoever”. Pyongyang continues to claim that the KAL flight was voluntarily diverted by the pilots and that the passengers who currently remain in North Korea do so by their own will. Neither of these claims have been verified.


Team Hwang believes in upholding justice and humanitarian principles that have been agreed upon by the international community, including North Korea.
